# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

CareBridge Companion is an open source project centered on youth safety, dignity, privacy, and respectful collaboration. We expect project spaces to remain constructive, inclusive, and professional.

This code of conduct applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, review comments, and other project-managed communication channels.

## Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- Communicate respectfully and assume good faith where reasonable.
- Focus discussion on the work, the evidence, and the impact of decisions.
- Welcome contributors with different backgrounds, experiences, and skill levels.
- Use language that protects dignity, especially when discussing youth, trauma, disability, religion, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, gender identity, or other protected characteristics.
- Accept corrective feedback when language, assumptions, or proposals could cause harm.
- Be careful not to disclose sensitive personal, facility, or security information.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory, demeaning, or exclusionary language
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- Trolling, hostility, baiting, or repeated bad-faith argument
- Doxxing or sharing private or identifying information without permission
- Posting confidential data, credentials, or security details irresponsibly
- Using project spaces to advocate unsafe, abusive, or exploitative treatment of youth

## Additional Expectations For This Project

Because this project operates in a sensitive domain, contributors must also:

- Avoid stigmatizing, dehumanizing, or sensational language about youth in care.
- Avoid dismissing concerns about safeguarding, bias, access control, or privacy.
- Treat policy-sensitive topics with care, evidence, and restraint.
- Respect that maintainers may reject technically workable changes if the safety or dignity tradeoff is not acceptable.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it to the maintainers through GitHub repository contact channels. If the concern is security-related, follow the process in `SECURITY.md` instead of posting publicly.

When reporting, include:

- What happened
- Where it happened
- Who was involved
- Any relevant links or screenshots
- Whether there is an immediate safety or privacy concern

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action they believe is necessary to protect the project and its participants, including:

- Requesting a change in behavior
- Editing, hiding, or deleting content
- Closing issues or pull requests
- Removing a contributor from discussions
- Temporarily or permanently restricting participation

Enforcement decisions will consider severity, pattern of behavior, impact on participants, and whether the behavior created safety, privacy, or discrimination risk.

## Scope And Interpretation

This code of conduct is intended to support a healthy project community. It does not replace facility safeguarding policies, employment policies, or legal reporting obligations that may apply outside the repository.

Maintainers may update this policy as the project matures.
