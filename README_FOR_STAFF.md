# CareBridge Companion ‚Äî A Simple Guide for Our Team

## What Is CareBridge Companion?

CareBridge Companion is a supportive AI chatbot tool that helps young people in our care talk about their feelings and concerns **24/7**, in a safe and private space. It's like having a caring listener available anytime‚Äîbefore bed, after a difficult conversation, or whenever a youth needs to talk.

This tool is designed for **all youth**, regardless of their background, immigration status, or life experience. Whether a young person is new to the country, learning English, or carrying experiences that are unique to their migration journey, this system is here to support them with respect and understanding.

The system then helps our care team by:
- Creating short summaries of what youth are experiencing
- Alerting us to safety concerns that need immediate attention
- Helping us plan better support and follow-ups

## How Does It Work?

### **Step 1: Youth Chat with the Companion**
A young person opens the app and talks to the companion‚Äîlike texting a caring friend. They can share whatever is on their mind.

### **Step 2: The System Listens & Learns**
The companion:
- Listens carefully to what's said
- Identifies the youth's mood and concerns
- Watches for safety red flags (like talk of self-harm or abuse)

### **Step 3: Staff Get Alerts & Summaries**
We receive:
- **Daily briefing**: "What was this youth's mood? What's on their mind?"
- **Safety alert**: "This message suggests they might be at risk‚Äîcheck in now"
- **Weekly summary**: Trends and patterns we should know about

### **Step 4: We Respond**
Staff check on the youth in person, document what we find, and follow our normal safety procedures. The system helps but doesn't replace us‚Äî**we stay in charge of all decisions.**

## Key Features Explained Simply

### **Facility Schedules**
The system respects our facility's routine. The companion is available:
- During the day and evening activities
- Before bedtime
- NOT during lights out (unless the facility chooses otherwise)

This helps youth build healthy habits and prevents overuse.

### **Safety Alerts**
If a youth mentions something concerning‚Äîlike thoughts of hurting themselves, concerns about abuse, or being in immediate danger‚Äî**we get notified right away.**

You'll see a message like:
> "‚ö†Ô∏è Sarah's message suggests she might be thinking about self-harm. Check in with her now."

Then you follow our normal safety protocols. You're trained for this‚Äîthe system just helps you notice faster.

### **Briefings (Summaries)**
Instead of reading every chat message, you get a short summary:
- **Mood trend**: "Getting more anxious over the past week"
- **Main themes**: "Worried about family visit" or "Peer conflict with roommate"
- **What to do**: "Check in today; consider a call to mom before the visit"

### **Privacy & Confidentiality**
- Youth's chats are **private** (conversations are between them and the companion)
- Staff briefings include the **minimum information** needed to help
- Identity-sensitive information (like LGBTQ+ disclosures, family separation concerns, or immigration status) is handled with extra care and confidentiality
- Immigration-related disclosures are protected and kept separate from any official documentation without the youth's consent
- Everything is encrypted and secure

## How to Respond to an Alert

### **When You Get a Safety Alert:**

**1. Stay calm** ‚Äî The system is helping, not panicking

**2. Check in with the youth in person** ‚Äî This is the most important step
   - Go talk to them face-to-face
   - Use phrases like: "I heard you might be struggling. I'm here to listen."
   - Be patient if language is a barrier‚Äîuse translation tools if needed
   - Show genuine care and respect for their unique experiences and background

**3. Follow our facility's safety procedures**
   - Alert your supervisor if needed
   - Escalate to the on-call clinician
   - Document what you observe and do

**4. Don't investigate alone**
   - If there are concerns about abuse or exploitation, involve your safeguarding team
   - Keep it professional and follow mandatory reporting rules

**5. Let supervisors know what happened**
   - Keep them updated so the next shift knows

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will the youth know what information we have?**
A: No. The youth knows they're talking to the companion, but they don't see what summaries we get.

**Q: What if the youth doesn't want to use it?**
A: Participation is voluntary. We never force a youth to chat. If they prefer not to, that's okay.

**Q: Does this replace my job?**
A: Absolutely not. You know these youth. The system helps you care better by providing information, but **you make all the decisions.**

**Q: What if I think the system is wrong?**
A: Trust your judgment. If you think an alert is a false alarm, check the youth anyway. Your in-person assessment is what matters most.

**Q: Is this HIPAA-compliant?**
A: Yes. Our IT team made sure all data is protected and secure. Talk to your supervisor for specific details.

**Q: What if a youth asks me about their chats?**
A: You can say: "I see summaries of how you're doing so I can help you better. Your private conversations are between you and the companion." You can also assure them: "You can talk about anything‚Äîyour feelings, your family, your background, your worries‚Äîand it stays private."

**Q: Can I read the full chat messages?**
A: Usually no‚Äîyou get summaries only. This protects youth privacy. In rare cases, supervisors can access full messages if there's a safety concern and proper authorization.
